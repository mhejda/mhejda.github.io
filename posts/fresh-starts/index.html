Fresh Starts: Blog edition

New blogging platform, new me.
August 30, 2022 · 3 min · 428 words · Matěj | Suggest Changes

I have always really liked the idea of a personal blog - imagining it as one’s very own tiny island in the vast seas of the global Internet network.

My family and some friends will surely remember many of my previous attempts at blogging. Way back in 2006, prior to the domain and brand being acquired by the current media company, I actually used to blog on Vox.com. I also owned multiple Wordpress instances, a Blogspot instance, and very likely at least few additional services. My very last attempt at blogging ended right when I started my undergrad in 2013 - and for almost 10 long years, I did not blog at all. In these years, I have instead started a digital journal using the DayOne iOS app. Those were the times when significant shift towards social media started to materialize - however, I have never really grown too keen on actually sharing things on these network. Data ownership & privacy are things that I care about, and everytime I opened Facebook, the experience was rather unpleasant - high ratio of noise (ads) to signal (friends), overall lacklustre experience and the thought “you are not the client, you are the product” would resonate through my mind, further intensified by situations such as the Cambridge Analytica scandal etc.

However, my online presence hiatus has changed last year, when I finally randomly found my own perfect blogging solution:

  • static blogging platform Hugo (free! + open-source)

  • with media hosted on Cloudinary CDN (free tier available),

  • the option to deploy it on Github Pages (free!)

  • and distribute via Netlify CDN (free!).

Since the content (except for media) is hosted on Github, this blog is also likely the first solution that will be permanent. 👀 Devon Zuegel once wrote a really cool article named Everything is Amazing, But Nothing is Ours, highlighting some of the developments in the ways we use and build our internet services, going from files to databases to increase convenience at the cost of dependencies. I have to say that going against the trend with a static platform like Hugo feels great - it’s so simple, and it works so well. There’s something about having one’s blog completely database and bloat-free, in both a local+remote git repository. Moving this to another server or even self-hosted instance should therefore be a breeze.

I feel confident that this platform will work (and last) for years ahead. My next task is to add a simple newsletter for those who will want to follow the updates posted here.

Thanks for reading me! :)
