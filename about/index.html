My main research interest is in practically feasible optical computing (OC) enabled by integrated photonics. I am primarily interested in architectures, systems and the required software stack for OC. Some of my focus topics include programmable photonic integrated circuits (PICs) in the context of photonic neural networks, hardware-software co-design and model translation layers for optical accelerators, and neuro-inspired photonic-electronic information processing. My past projects include work functionalization of 2D nanomaterials (at Czech Academy of Sciences) and development of interferometric 3D imaging systems with subnanometric axial resolution (at ICFO), contributing to research which later led to a spin-off company called ShinePhi.

Driven by curiosity and aiming for real world impact, I deeply enjoy working on challenging problems spanning diverse fields and disciplines, especially at the bleeding edge of technology and with practical application in sight. Besides my core knowledge and skills in (experimental) photonics, I enjoy all kinds of programming, data visualizations and machine learning (PyTorch). I have experience working as a technical contributor for large European (EIC/HORIZON) research projects.
